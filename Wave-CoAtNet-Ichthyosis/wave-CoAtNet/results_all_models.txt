================================================================================
  KEY CLAIMS FOR PAPER
================================================================================
1. WaveCoAtNet achieves 89.24% accuracy, competitive with Swin-T (89.87%),
   CoAtNet (89.87%), and DINOv2 (89.87%) on the held-out test set.

2. WaveCoAtNet uses 30.1M parameters -- 2.85x fewer than ViT-B/16 (85.8M)
   and 6.5x fewer than BiomedCLIP (195.9M) while matching their accuracy.

3. The novel modules (WG-FDCA, PA-DTS, SCTR) add only 2.3M parameters
   over the ConvNeXt-Tiny baseline (27.8M -> 30.1M) while improving
   accuracy by +1.27% and macro F1 by +3.78%.

4. All accuracy differences between top-7 pretrained models are within
   1.27% on 158 test samples and are not statistically significant
   (McNemar p > 0.37), underscoring the need for 5-fold cross-validation.

5. Transfer learning is critical: pretrained models outperform scratch
   counterparts by 11-16% accuracy, confirming that ImageNet features
   transfer effectively to dermatological image classification.

6. Scratch models consistently fail on minority classes (Lamellar F1:
   0.15-0.34, Netherton F1: 0.40-0.48), while pretrained models achieve
   Lamellar F1: 0.59-0.73 and Netherton F1: 0.60-0.77.

7. Among all pretrained models, WaveCoAtNet achieves the highest
   Healthy Skin F1 (98.90%), tied with BiomedCLIP.

8. Lamellar ichthyosis remains the hardest class for ALL models
   (pretrained F1 range: 58.82%-72.73%; scratch F1 range: 15.38%-34.29%),
   reflecting genuine clinical difficulty in distinguishing this subtype.
